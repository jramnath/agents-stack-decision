Where this is all heading

Most teams build like it is still 2024: they pick LangGraph before they know if they need state, add a vector database before they outgrow Postgres, and design multi-agent architectures before shipping one agent that works. The gap is not talent or budget. It is knowing which layers matter for your specific agent instead of half-building all six.

The stack is going to collapse. Provider SDKs are already absorbing memory, tool calling, and basic eval into a single API. By early 2027, most teams will take an opinionated stack from their model provider and that will be fine for ~80% of use cases. The other 20% — agents at scale where the defaults break — will still build custom at every layer. Even then, when something fails in production, you need to know which layer failed. That is what these three steps are for.
